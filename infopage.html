Favourite Poem

It was hard to believe the flesh was heavy on my back.
The fisherman said: Will you have her, carelessly,
trailing his nets and his nerves, as though his words
sanctified the purpose with which the faced himself.
I saw his white bone thrash his eyes.

I followed him across the sprawling sands,
my mind thumping in the flesh's sling.
Hope lay perhaps in burning the house I lived in.
silence gripped my sleeves;his body clawed at the froth
his old nets had only dragged up from the seas

In the flickering dark his lean to opened like a wound.
The wind was I,and the days and nights before.
Palm fronds scratched my skin. Inside the shark
an oil lamp splayed the hours bunched to those walls.
over and over the sticky soot croosed the space of my mind.

I heard him say: My daughter, she's just turned fifteen...
Feel her: I'll be back soon,your bus leaves at nine.
The sky fell on me, and a father's exhausted wile.
Long and lean,her years were cold as rubber.
